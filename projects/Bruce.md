[Bruce Firmware (v1.13)](https://bruce.computer) is a multi-purpose firmware designed for wireless experimentation and radio frequency analysis using compact hardware devices.

The goal of this project is to test a real application of wireless vulnerabilities studied in these past months.

---

## Hardware Platform

The experiments are conducted using the **C1101 Plus**, a portable device designed for radio experimentation built on:

- **ESP32-S3** – sub-GHz transceiver used for many ISM band devices
- **NRF24L01** – 2.4 GHz transceiver widely used in low-power wireless communication
- additional hardware interfaces depending on the device configuration

Follow this [link](https://lilygo.cc/en-us/products/t-embed-cc1101-plus?srsltid=AfmBOorgC1g1iTq5aulf6SSfiIF4HyQerCXGnMwhzie0mKJBW7ovnXFg) if you want to know more!

Another hardware in my possession that works with this firmware is M5StickCplus2, but there are so many others also buildable from scratch.

## First Module: NRF24L01

![NRF24](https://nicofragale.github.io/assets/images/menu0.png) 

The first module I want to introduce is the **NRF24L01**, a popular 2.4 GHz transceiver chip developed by Nordic Semiconductor.

![modulo](https://nicofragale.github.io/assets/images/modulo.png) 

As shown in the image below, the module comes with a **very small integrated antenna**.  
Despite its size, it is capable of interacting with devices operating in the crowded 2.4 GHz ISM band.

![modulo1](https://nicofragale.github.io/assets/images/modulo1.png) 

To test its capabilities, I performed a simple experiment using an **iPhone connected to a Bluetooth speaker** in home.

When activating the NRF24L01 module and positioning myself **between the two devices**, the interference generated by the transmitter was sufficient to disturb the communication channel; in this situation the speaker either stopped playing audio temporarily or the music became noticeably distorted.

However, when I moved away from the line of communication and stood **behind either the speaker or the phone**, the interference effect disappeared and the audio transmission continued normally.

### Antenna Enhancement

In order to improve the reception capabilities of the module, I decided to modify the device by replacing the **integrated antenna** with an external one purchased from AliExpress.

To do this, I carefully opened the holes on the back of the device, which appear to be intentionally designed to allow an antenna to be mounted and connected to the internal module, as shown in the image below.

![antenna](https://nicofragale.github.io/assets/images/c1antenna.jpg)

After installing the new antenna, I repeated the same experiment described earlier and the results showed a **slight improvement in the interference range**, but the difference was not as significant as expected. The signal disturbance still occurs when positioned between the two devices, but the overall impact remains relatively limited.

There are several possible explanations for this behavior:

- the **output transmission power of the NRF24L01 module** is relatively low  
- the antenna purchased may not be **properly tuned for 2.4 GHz**
- the device's **internal RF design or impedance matching** might limit the antenna's effectiveness
- Bluetooth uses **frequency hopping**, which makes sustained interference more difficult

For the next iteration, I plan to build a **custom jammer from scratch** and compare its performance with the current configuration to better understand how antenna design affects the module's behavior.
